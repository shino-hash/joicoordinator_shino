Meet Your Japan Outreach Coordinator

Hello! I'm Shino Manabe, the Japan Outreach Initiative Program Coordinator at Utah State University. I came to Logan, Utah to share the beauty of Japanese culture with the local community.

My journey with Japanese culture began with my grandmother, who taught calligraphy and flower arranging. I grew up surrounded by seasonal flowers at our home and learned to find calm and focus through calligraphy.

My travels to Indonesia, where I introduced Japanese culture to new audiences for the first time in my life, deepened my appreciation for its richness.

Through workshops, presentations, and interactive activities, I aim to inspire curiosity and make Japanese culture accessible and engaging for everyone.

Program Offerings

I offer a variety of programs and workshops tailored to different audiences and interests. My goal is to make learning about Japan fun and accessible for everyone.

Hands-on Cultural Workshops

Experience hands-on activities like origami, Japanese calligraphy (shodo), traditional wrapping with furoshiki, or learning to use chopsticks.
